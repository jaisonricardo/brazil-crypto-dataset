# The Escalation of Deepfakes and Crypto Frauds in Brazil: Analysis of the Legal Response and Strategic Challenges in the Face of Artificial Intelligence

**Author:** Jaison Sfogia Ricardo  
**Published in:** Editora Científica  
**Year:** 2026  
**Language:** English  

## Abstract
The exponential advancement of artificial intelligence (AI) has driven the proliferation of deepfakes—ultra-realistic synthetic multimedia content—which, combined with the pseudonymity of cryptoassets, constitutes a growing vector for financial fraud and cybercrime. Schemes such as pig butchering, rug pulls, market manipulation, and identity fraud are amplified by AI, resulting in global financial losses that have reached USD 79.1 billion since 2022. A study by Sumsub (2024) places Brazil among the top ten countries with the highest number of deepfake-based attacks, with a significant increase in occurrences during the analyzed period, particularly in the cryptocurrency segment.

In light of this complexity, Brazil has adapted its legislation, notably through Law No. 14,478/2022, which regulates virtual asset service providers (VASPs), enhances anti-money laundering and counter-terrorist financing measures (AML/CFT), and introduces the criminal offense of fraud involving virtual assets (Article 171-A of the Brazilian Penal Code). This article critically analyzes the evolution and impacts of deepfakes and crypto frauds, the Brazilian legislative and operational response, and the remaining challenges in mitigating these risks, proposing strategic measures to strengthen national resilience.
