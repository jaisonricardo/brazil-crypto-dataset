# Cryptoasset Governance and Regulatory Compliance: Global Challenges in the Prevention of Digital Financial Crimes

**Author:** Jaison Sfogia Ricardo  
**Published in:** Periódico REASE  
**Year:** 2026  
**Language:** English  

## Abstract
The advent and rapid rise of cryptoassets have transformed the global financial landscape, fostering innovation while also introducing substantial challenges in combating financial crimes, notably money laundering (ML) and terrorist financing (TF). This article explores the complexities of the decentralized and pseudonymous nature of cryptoassets, which are exploited for criminal purposes, culminating in annual global money laundering volumes estimated between USD 800 billion and 2 trillion.

Through an analysis of the Brazilian regulatory framework (Law No. 14,478/2022 and regulations issued by the Central Bank of Brazil) and a comparison with international regulatory approaches (FATF, the European Union, and the United States), this study identifies regulatory gaps and inconsistencies. It proposes a critical discussion on the effectiveness of existing measures, particularly in light of the technical limitations of asset freezing and the persistence of fund movements by sanctioned entities.

Finally, it outlines future strategies, emphasizing international cooperation, investment in advanced technologies such as blockchain analytics, artificial intelligence (AI), and embedded supervision, as well as the need for a strategic balance between fostering innovation and ensuring financial security and integrity in the digital age.
